MAKING LIFE MUSICAL

Baja Nepal is a common digital platform to connect the traditional musicians, their skills and conserve the traditional instruments while providing economic opportunity to those who are skilled yet unemployed.

Baja Nepal is an idea about giving oppurtinity to underprivilegde communities from mechi to makahali. It is the common digital platform to connect the traditional bajauney( musicians) to different events like wedding , ceremonies , welcome programms , bratabandha etc.

About Us

Baja Nepal is a simple idea originated during the discussion of four friends of Media Lab Nepal on tackling the problem of unemployment. Idea is about creating the employment oppurtinity to the traditional musicians of the nepal. This helps to solve the problems of the customers (not getting the oppurtinity to use the traditional music ) by connecting the user and the bajaunyes through the single digital platform . unexpectedly, the idea became a concrete plan and helped us to win Hult Prize Purbanchal University 2018/19 setting our journey to Malaysia for Regional competition of Hult prize worth of $1million, also known as Nobel Prize for students. The idea impressed the judges and audiences with zero controversy. Idea is also supported by Lok Baja Sangrahalaya , Nach Ghar , Nepal Tourism Board etc.

In present context, thousands of people are moving abroad in the search of jobs. Lack of skill might be one case but in many cases, platform and connection is the major cause of unemployment. Our initiative targets those underprivileged skilled manpower who are mainly found in Nepali Dalit communities. Their skills’ admiration is found in village areas but they are totally disconnected to city areas so the last generation has started leaving their profession leading to decline in Nepali tradition and musical instruments. We believe that they are the true musicians. They have got legacy born talent. But is their talent benefitting them? BajaNepal is a common digital platform to connect the traditional bajauney( musicians) to different events like wedding ceremonies, welcome programmes, bratabandha etc. occurring in city areas. The platform is thought to be supportive for traditional musical instruments conservation and to solve the problem of such skilled unemployed people of remote areas. The initiative will be first of its kind in the world so has the potential to represent Nepal and Nepali traditions proudly in the international competition. The journey to Malaysia is one of the strategy to do the marketing of Nepali traditions, traditional musicians and the unique initiative we are continuing. Our initative is a social business more than just a business. It works on a minimum commission basis for our sustainability with an aim to provide quality and affordable team of traditional musicians.
